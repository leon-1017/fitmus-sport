<div class="fusion-title title fusion-title-size-two" style="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:31px;"><h2 class="title-heading-left">Feature</h2><div class="title-sep-container"><div class="title-sep sep-double sep-solid" style="border-color:#e03420;"></div></div></div><div class="fusion-text"><p>Football Speed Hurdle normally&nbsp;come in sets of 12 and are the following heights 6”, 9” or 12”. Be sure to evaluate your athletes (or yourself) and choose a height that is appropriate. You may also want to consider using less than the full set of 12 for beginners.</p>
<p>Hurdle set-up: The best way to evenly space out your hurdles is to use the width of a hurdle as your measuring stick.<br>
Who: Any athlete (kids love these!). Runners Feed suggests teaching the A-March and A-Skip prior to practicing mini-hurdle drills<br>
Where: preferably on a track, turf, or well-groomed grass field<br>
When: Anytime following a light cardio warm up.<br>
Why: to improve biomechanical efficiency and to increase turnover (the amount of steps one takes per second), agility, quick knee lift, and body awareness.<br>
Ideally one coach will be actively correctly form and technique while another coach/athlete will be on “hurdle fixing duty”.</p>
