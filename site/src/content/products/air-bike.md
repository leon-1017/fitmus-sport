<div class="fusion-title title fusion-title-size-two" style="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:31px;"><h2 class="title-heading-left">Feature</h2><div class="title-sep-container"><div class="title-sep sep-double sep-solid" style="border-color:#e03420;"></div></div></div><div class="fusion-text"><p>In CrossFit, cardio-based exercises can include running, swimming, rowing, and now…. the Air Bike&nbsp; If running or rowing is currently something you are unable to do due to injury, the &nbsp;Air Bike is a great way to modify that movement in your workout. If you have a rolled ankle or a quad or hip flexor strain, the Air Bike provides a good option for exercising safely. It could potentially be a great tool for rehabilitation as it provides safe movement with no impact. It also allows the area of the injury to move and provide blood flow to the area to aid in recovery.</p>
<p>The &nbsp;Air Bike is also great for metabolic conditioning, as it can help break through plateaus in your training. If your aerobic capacity is not feeling as if it has improved, the Air Bike is a great way to incorporate interval training to build your aerobic capacity without losing strength and muscle mass [1][2].</p>
<p>The &nbsp;Air Bike can also enhance your mindset. If you have ever used the Air Bike at high intensity, you would agree that it is not easy. The Air Bike is a great test of mental toughness and how to sustain a positive mindset through pain.</p>
