CrossFit can feel like a new relationship at first: new movements, quick progress and a supportive community. Plateaus are normal, however. When benchmark times stop improving or a lift stalls, use the following principles to rekindle progress:

- **Listen to your body.** Distinguish normal fatigue from pain that may indicate injury, and keep technique ahead of load.
- **Share a realistic goal.** A coach and training partners can provide accountability, but progress should be measured in achievable steps.
- **Use a progression.** A well-designed programme makes volume, technique and adaptation visible; work with a coach on weaknesses.
- **Take rest.** A planned break or accessory block can provide a new stimulus and let the body recover.
- **Trust small steps.** An extra kilogram or a second less in a workout is meaningful progress.
