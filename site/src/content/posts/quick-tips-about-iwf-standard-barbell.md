<p><strong>What is IWF?</strong></p>
<p>The International Weightlifting Federation (IWF) is a permanent non-profit organisation composed of 189 affiliated National Federations worldwide, from all five continents.Weightlifting was one of the first sports included already in the first Modern Olympic Games 1896.The IWF was founded in 1905, making it one of the oldest International Sport Federations.The IWF is the world controlling body for weightlifting, recognised by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) as the sole controlling body for international weightlifting.The IWF is a member of SportAccord and the Association of Summer Olympic International Federations (ASOIF).</p>
<p>The International Weightlifting Federation (IWF) is the world controlling body for weightlifting<br>
As a permanent non-profit organization composed of 189 affiliated National Federations worldwide, from all five Continents, the IWF is recognized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) as the sole controlling body for International Weightlifting<br>
The IWF aims to promote Weightlifting as a core sport &amp; support the development, health and unity for the benefit of all its stakeholders<br>
Missions<br>
To promote, lead and govern the sport of weightlifting throughout the world<br>
To endorse the key values of the sport and the Olympic principles<br>
To support all initiatives for peace and understanding<br>
To advocate and ensure competitions in the spirit of Fair Play<br>
To provide equal conditions for different Continents, countries, individuals without distinctions for reasons of race, color, gender, religion or politics</p>
<p><strong>What is IWF Barbell?</strong></p>
<p>Only barbells meeting <strong>IWF specifications</strong> and approval may be used in weightlifting competitions under its jurisdiction.</p>
<p><strong>What is IWF Specification Barbell?</strong></p>

the following authorized specifications:<br>
IWF TCR 3.3.3.3<br>

Regulation to 3.3.3</p>

<p>2.2 Discs<br>
IWF TCR 3.3.3.5<br>
Discs<br>
Two types of discs are authorised for use by the IWF: Competition and Training discs.<br>
Both types of discs must meet the following authorized specifications:<br>
IWF TCR 3.3.3.6<br>
Competition discs: (weights in kilograms with corresponding colour)<br>
30 kg black*<br>
25 kg red<br>
20 kg blue<br>
15 kg yellow<br>
10 kg green<br>
5 kg white<br>
2.5 kg red<br>
2 kg blue<br>
1.5 kg yellow<br>
1 kg green<br>
0.5 kg white<br>
*Note: Details of the introduction of 30 kg discs are subject to further co-ordination.</p>
<p>Regulation to 3.3.3<br>
Competition discs:<br>
a) Diameter<br>
• Discs ≥ 10 kg : 45 cm ± 0.1 cm tolerance<br>
• Discs &lt; 10 kg ≤ 45 cm ± 0.1 cm tolerance<br>
b) Material<br>
• Discs ≥ 10 kg: covered with rubber or plastic and coated on both sides with<br>
permanent colour<br>
• Discs &lt; 10 kg: may be made of metal or other IWF approved material<br>
c) Tolerance<br>
• Discs &gt; 5 kg: from +0.1% to -0.05%<br>
• Discs ≤ 5 kg: from +10 grams to -0 grams per part<br>
d) Marking<br>
• all discs must be clearly demarcated with the weight in kilograms<br>
IWF TCR 3.3.3.7<br>
Training discs<br>
• manufactured in coloured weights as listed above<br>
• may be manufactured in black with corresponding coloured rims and demarcated<br>
with “Training”<br>
Regulation to 3.3.3<br>
Training discs<br>
• tolerance = from +0.8% to -0.8%<br>
• manufactured in colours with corresponding weight listed in TCR 3.3.3.6<br>
• may be manufactured in black with corresponding coloured rims and demarcated<br>
with “Training”</p>
<p>2.3 Collars<br>
IWF TCR 3.3.3.8<br>
Collars<br>
In order to secure the discs to the bar, each bar must be equipped with collars. Collars<br>
must meet the following authorised specifications:<br>
• two (2) collars per barbell<br>
• weight = 2.5 kg each<br>
Regulation to 3.3.3<br>
Collars<br>
• manufactured from chromed steel<br>
• hole = 5 cm diameter<br>
• may be designed for either outside loading or inside loading for 2 kg or less<br>
• tolerance = from +10 grams to -0 grams per part<br>

<p>3.) SCALES<br>
IWF TCR 3.3.4.1<br>
At World Championships, Olympic Games and other select IWF Events, multiple<br>
identical scales are required.<br>
Regulation to 3.3.4<br>
Scales must:<br>
• be electronic; with display and/or printout or both<br>
• capacity = weigh up to 200 kg<br>
• precision = 10 grams minimum<br>
• minimum three (3) scales – official scale, test scale and training venue scale<br>
• certified by local authorities within three (3) months from start of the Event<br>
• Olympic Games, Youth Olympic Games – scales must be calibrated daily</p>
