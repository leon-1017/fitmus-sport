Squats are a powerful full-body lift, but flexibility limits, injuries or weak glutes can make alternatives useful. The original article highlights these options:

1. **Dumbbell step-ups** — step onto a box while keeping the chest tall; useful when back or shoulder issues make a barbell squat uncomfortable.
2. **Trap-bar deadlifts** — combine a knee-dominant movement with a hip hinge and build the posterior chain.
3. **Sled pushes and reverse drags** — train the glutes, hamstrings and quads with relatively simple technique.
4. **Glute-ham raises** — target the posterior chain through controlled hip and knee extension.
5. **Single-leg variations** — challenge balance and leg strength independently while allowing load and range to be adjusted.

Choose the variation that matches your movement quality and current limitations. Use light loads while learning technique and seek qualified advice for pain or injury.
