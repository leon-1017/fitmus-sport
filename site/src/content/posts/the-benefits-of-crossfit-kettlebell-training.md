Kettlebell training combines strength and conditioning in a compact format. The offset load asks the body to stabilise through different positions rather than isolating one muscle group.

## Three benefits

1. **Total-body strength.** A double-kettlebell rack squat trains the hamstrings, glutes and core while the lifter braces through the trunk.
2. **Higher calorie demand.** Swings use the hips and stabilising muscles together; keep the knees softly bent without turning the movement into a deep squat.
3. **Improved balance.** A bottoms-up press challenges control and stability. Begin with low repetitions and increase only as the position becomes comfortable.

Learn each movement with a manageable load and prioritise technique before intensity.
