<p><a href="/products/power-rack-cage/" target="_blank">Power Cage&nbsp;</a>is the most important piece of gym equipment. But many gyms don’t have one. Building a home gym is often the only solution.</p>
<p>Benefits of Power Cage.<img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-3027" src="/images/2016/10/commerical-power-rack-300x300.jpg" alt="commerical-power-rack" width="300" height="300" data-orig-sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></p>
<p><a href="/products/weight-lifting/" target="_blank">Free weights</a> build more muscle and strength than machines. Work stations like Bowflex will give you subpar results and don’t work for routines like StrongLifts 5×5.</p>
<p>Safety. Power Cage have lateral <a href="/product/cross-fit-rig-safety-pin/" target="_blank">safety pins</a> to catch the barbell in case something goes wrong. You can lift safely without a spotter.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-3035" src="/images/2016/10/Commerical-power-rack-1-300x300.jpg" alt="commerical-power-rack-1" width="300" height="300" data-orig-sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></p>
<p>Easier. Squats are key to building muscle &amp; strength. But without Power Rack you’ll struggle to get the bar on your back for Squats.</p>
<p>Versatile. Tons of exercises you can do: Squat, Bench Press, Inverted Rows, Dips, Pull-ups, Chin-ups, Rack Pulls and many more. Drawbacks of Power Rack. In terms of results, buying a Power Rack is the best decision you can make. But there are drawbacks: Takes Space. Your basement or garage needs to be big enough for your Power Rack to fit in. Especially your ceiling must be high.</p>
<p>Needs Extra Equipment. You need a barbell, bench &amp;<a href="/product/premium-color-olympic-rubber-bumper-plates/" target="_blank"> plates</a> if you want to do as many exercises as you can with your Power Rack. Costs More Money. Because you must buy extra equipment on top of it.</p>
<p>How to Choose a Power Rack.</p>
<p>The higher the quality and the more options, the more expensive the Power Rack. Things you must look for: Sturdy. Quality construction with little plastic. Load capacity of minimum 1000lbs/455kg. Power rack shouldn’t be too light.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2398" src="/images/2016/04/Power-cage-300x180.jpg" alt="Power cage" width="300" height="180" data-orig-sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></p>
<p>Size. Tall enough so you can Overhead Press inside of it. Wide enough for increased stability and so you can do exercises like Sumo Squats.</p>
<p><a href="/product/upright-bar/" target="_blank">Uprights</a>. Adjustable to many heights and with narrow spacing. Outside uprights so you can Press outside of your rack if it’s too short.</p>
<p>Safety Pins. Removable or adjustable to all the way down so you can Floor Press inside your Power Rack. Narrow spacing for pin work.</p>
<p>Pull-up &amp; Dip Bar. For Pull-ups, Chin-ups and Dips. Best is a Power Rack with a straight Pull-up bar.</p>
