Training masks cover the mouth and nose and restrict airflow. Supporters claim they simulate altitude work and may challenge breathing during exercise, but it remains unclear whether they improve athletic performance in the same way as true altitude exposure.

Restrictive breathing can cause hyperventilation, dizziness or fainting. People with high blood pressure or cardiovascular problems should avoid such equipment, and anyone using a mask should stop if symptoms appear.
