<p>BEIJING, Aug. 7 (Xinhua) — China will soon launch a new round of central environmental inspections covering eight provincial-level areas, as the country finishes nationwide investigations into local environmental protection efforts, according to an official statement Monday.</p>
<p>A total of eight inspection teams will start month-long on-site investigations into local government work, including Jilin, Zhejiang and Shandong provinces, the Ministry of Environmental Protection (MEP) said in a statement.</p>
<p>Inspectors will monitor key environmental issues, oversee local improvements and push local government accountability.</p>
<p>China launched a series of central environmental inspections in 2016 after decades of growth left the country saddled with problems such as smog and contaminated soil.</p>
<p>During the last round of inspections into seven provincial areas, China held over 4,660 officials accountable for poor environmental protection work.</p>
<p>The teams have found common problems, including illegal projects in nature reserves and a lack of eco-friendly facilities.</p>
<p>Last year, inspectors looked into 33,000 cases, imposing fines totalling 440 million yuan (65.4 million U.S. dollars). A total of 720 people were detained and almost 6,500 people held accountable.</p>
