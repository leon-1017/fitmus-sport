Competition kettlebells keep the same external dimensions and handle geometry as the weight changes. That consistent forearm position and hand insertion helps athletes repeat technique with 8 kg, 18 kg or 32 kg bells.

They are made from steel, with handles designed for chalk and reduced lateral slipping. Their colour coding makes weights easy to identify, and the uniform shape suits technical movements such as Turkish get-ups and high-repetition snatches.
